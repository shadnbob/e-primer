Opinion Words - Three Intensity Levels

Mild (Level 1): This possibly indicates a trend. It seems like the data suggests a pattern. This might be significant, though it's arguably too early to tell.
Moderate (Level 2): This clearly shows a problem. The evidence obviously points to one conclusion. We certainly need to act, as this definitely requires attention.
Severe (Level 3): This is absolutely unacceptable. The facts are undeniably clear. This is incontrovertibly the worst outcome. The evidence is irrefutably damning.

Hover over any highlighted word to see its intent analysis, psychological effect, and suggested alternatives.

Emotional Manipulation - Escalating Intensity

Mild: This is concerning news that seems troubling. It's an important issue that could be meaningful for our community.
Moderate: We face a dangerous situation. This serious threat is shocking and outrageous. The devastating impact cannot be ignored.
Severe: This existential threat will have catastrophic consequences. Those responsible have blood on their hands. We must think of the children whose innocent lives are in grave danger.

Gaslighting Phrases - Manipulation Severity

Mild: You might be mistaken about that detail. Perhaps you misunderstood what was said. Are you sure that's what happened?
Moderate: You're overreacting to this situation. That's not what happened at all. You're being sensitive about a minor issue.
Severe: You're imagining things that never occurred. You're crazy if you believe that. That never happened - you're delusional if you think it did.

Combined Example with Multiple Intensities

This report clearly shows an important trend. Critics who say that never happened are absolutely wrong. We face a serious threat that requires immediate action. Some might argue otherwise, but the evidence is irrefutably clear. Those who deny this existential threat are either delusional or have blood on their hands. You're overreacting if you think this language is too strong - the situation is undeniably dire.

Hover Card Features

When you hover over highlighted text, you'll see:

  • Bias Type & Severity: Clear identification and intensity level
  • Detected Phrase: The exact text that triggered detection
  • Likely Intent: Why the writer might use this language
  • Psychological Effect: How it impacts readers' thinking
  • Neutral Alternative: More objective ways to express the same idea
  • Context: Other biases found nearby that might work together

Try hovering over this severely biased sentence: The absolutely catastrophic situation clearly demands we think of the children before it's too late.

Visual Hierarchy Guide

Dotted underline (Mild): Subtle bias that gently influences
Solid underline (Moderate): Clear bias that noticeably shapes perception
Thick underline + ⚠️ (Severe): Strong manipulation that demands critical evaluation

Background opacity also increases with severity, making more manipulative language more visually prominent.
