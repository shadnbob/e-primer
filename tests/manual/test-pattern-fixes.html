Pattern Fix Test - Verifying Word Boundaries

Testing "no" Pattern (Absolute Words)

Should Match:

No way! There is no evidence for that claim.

I have no idea what you mean.

No, I disagree with that statement.

Should NOT Match (word boundaries):

I know that already. Knowledge is power.

Unknown factors may affect the outcome.

Cannot determine the answer. Innovation is key.

It's snowing outside. The piano sounds beautiful.

Acknowledge the problem. Diagnose the issue.

Testing "seems" and "appears" (Opinion Words)

Should Match:

It seems like a good idea to me.

The solution appears to be working.

This seems reasonable. That appears correct.

Apparently, the situation has changed.

Seemingly, there are no other options.

Testing Intensity Levels:

Level 1 (Mild): It seems possible. This appears feasible.

Level 2 (Moderate): Obviously, this is the answer. Clearly wrong.

Level 3 (Severe): Undeniably true! Absolutely indisputable!

Testing Other Absolute Words

Should Match:

All people deserve respect. Every child needs education.

Any questions? None of the above. Each person is unique.

Should NOT Match:

Call me later. Really important. Beach vacation.

Reach out if needed. Many thanks. Canon camera.

Balloon festival. Preach to the choir.

Testing Excellence Patterns

According to Dr. Smith's 2024 study, the results appear promising. However, it seems we need more research. In my opinion, while the evidence suggests a trend, we should remain open to alternative interpretations.

Expected Results After Fix:
  • "no" should only highlight as a complete word, not in "know", "unknown", etc.
  • "seems" and "appears" should be highlighted as opinion words
  • All absolute words should respect word boundaries
  • Excellence patterns should still work correctly
