Probability Perception Language Test

Problematic Probability Language (Should be Highlighted)

This medication has minimal risk of side effects and is generally safe for most patients.

The procedure is highly unlikely to cause complications, with only rare side effects reported.

There's a remote possibility of problems, but patients usually recover without issues.

Studies suggest this treatment is very safe with negligible risk to healthy individuals.

The vaccine rarely causes serious reactions and occasionally leads to mild symptoms.

This investment strategy has low risk and typically performs well over time.

Climate change will probably affect weather patterns, though impacts may occur gradually.

The software update is quite likely to improve performance and has minimal risk of causing issues.

Housing prices will most probably increase, with only a slight possibility of decline.

Acceptable Probability Language (Should NOT be Highlighted)

This medication causes side effects in 5% of patients, with serious reactions occurring in 1 in 10,000 cases.

The procedure has a 2% complication rate based on data from 50,000 patients over 5 years.

Clinical trials show a 95% success rate (confidence interval: 92-98%) in controlled studies.

According to FDA data, adverse reactions occur in 3 out of 1,000 patients treated with this drug.

The vaccine causes fever in 12% of recipients and severe allergic reactions in 0.001% of cases.

Historical data shows this investment lost money in 15% of 10-year periods since 1950.

Climate models predict 2-4°C warming by 2100 with 95% confidence based on current emissions.

Beta testing with 1,000 users found bugs in 0.3% of installations requiring rollback.

Market analysis shows housing declined in 23% of years since 1970, with average annual growth of 3.2%.

Mixed Examples

While this treatment is generally safe, clinical data shows side effects occur in 8% of patients.

The risk appears minimal, though we lack specific statistics from randomized trials.

Evidence suggests most patients benefit, but 15% experience no improvement after 6 months.

This approach is typically successful in our experience, with documented success in 78% of similar cases.
