Bias Detector: Rhetorical Devices Test Page

False Balance Markers

These phrases create artificial equivalence between unequal positions:

The climate debate has both sides presenting equally valid arguments. On one hand, scientists claim warming is happening, but on the other hand, some skeptics disagree. It's important to maintain a balanced perspective on this controversial issue.

The ongoing debate about vaccines shows there are two schools of thought. To be fair, we should consider alternative viewpoints and recognize this is a matter of opinion. Playing devil's advocate, one could argue there are pros and cons to vaccination.

This divisive issue has supporters and critics on both sides. From different perspectives, the contentious matter looks quite different. We must examine the strengths and weaknesses of each position to find middle ground.

Note: False balance markers should be highlighted in indigo color.

Euphemisms and Dysphemisms

These are loaded terms that frame concepts positively or negatively:

Political/Military: The military operation involved enhanced interrogation techniques and resulted in some collateral damage. After the kinetic action, forces made a strategic withdrawal to avoid further friendly fire incidents.

Corporate: The company announced a rightsizing initiative as part of its restructuring plan. Despite negative growth and challenging market conditions, they're pursuing synergy realization through workforce adjustment.

Social: Many senior citizens in their golden years have passed away recently. The economically disadvantaged and differently abled individuals in our community need special needs support.

Dysphemisms: Critics call the estate tax a death tax and claim the healthcare bill is socialized medicine - a government takeover by the nanny state. They say these job killers come from tax and spend politicians.

Note: Euphemisms and dysphemisms should be highlighted in dark green color.

Combined Example

The ongoing debate about immigration reform shows clear divisions. On one hand, advocates for undocumented workers argue for compassion, while on the other hand, those concerned about illegal aliens worry about border security. This controversial issue involves questions about chain migration versus family reunification. Both sides have valid concerns in this polarizing topic. The government's enhanced border enforcement has been called both necessary security and a government takeover. Finding middle ground on this contentious matter requires examining the pros and cons of each approach.

This example contains both false balance markers (indigo) and euphemisms/dysphemisms (dark green).
