🔍 Basic Detection Category 1/4

Fundamental bias indicators that are enabled by default

Opinion Words Test

This content is obviously fantastic and clearly demonstrates the amazing capabilities of our system. It's definitely superior to other solutions and undoubtedly provides the best results. Everyone absolutely loves how brilliant and perfect this approach is. The interface is incredibly user-friendly and remarkably intuitive.

Quick Reference - One Word from Each Opinion Subcategory:

🎯 Certainty: clearly | ❓ Hedging: probably | 👍 Positive: excellent | 👎 Negative: poor | ⚡ Emotional: heartwarming | 📊 Comparative: best | 🏛️ Political: controversial | 🔥 Intensifiers: extremely | 🗣️ Credibility: claims | ⚖️ Loaded Political: freedom | ⚖️ Moral: immoral | 💭 Emotional Appeals: promising

Problematic Examples:

The policy is obviously flawed and terrible in every way. This solution is clearly the worst possible approach, and everyone knows it's completely useless. The results are definitely disappointing and absolutely catastrophic.

Acceptable Examples:

In my opinion, this approach shows promise. Dr. Smith considers this method effective based on her research. Critics argue that the implementation needs improvement, while supporters believe it addresses key concerns.

To-Be Verbs (E-Prime) Test

The weather is nice today, and the sky is blue. Our team is dedicated to excellence, and success is our primary goal. The problem is complex, but the solution is simple. Technology is the answer to most challenges, and innovation is key to progress.

Problematic Examples:

Success is hard work. The economy is immigrants. Happiness is money. Violence is never the answer. Government is the problem.

Acceptable Examples:

Water is H2O. The meeting is scheduled for 3 PM. The document is 50 pages long. The temperature is 72 degrees. The chemical formula is C6H12O6.

Absolute Statements Test

All politicians always lie about everything. Nobody ever tells the complete truth in politics. Everyone knows that nothing ever changes. Every single policy always fails completely. No one would ever disagree with this assessment.

Problematic Examples:

All teenagers are irresponsible. Everyone hates this policy. Nobody cares about the environment. Always leads to disaster. Never works in practice.

Acceptable Examples:

All triangles have three sides. Every participant signed the consent form. Always follow safety protocols. Never mix these chemicals. All data must be verified.

🧠 Advanced Detection Category 2/4

Sophisticated linguistic patterns that require deeper analysis

Passive Voice Test

Mistakes were made during the implementation process. Critical decisions were taken without proper consultation. The project was delayed due to unforeseen circumstances. Issues were identified but solutions weren't implemented. The budget was exceeded and deadlines were missed.

Problematic Examples:

Errors were discovered. The decision was made. Action will be taken. The policy was changed. Problems were encountered.

Acceptable Examples:

The samples were analyzed by Dr. Johnson. The experiment was conducted by the research team. Data was collected from 500 participants. The results were published in Nature.

Weasel Words Test

Experts believe that this approach will work. Studies show significant improvements in performance. Many people say the results are impressive. It is widely known that this method is effective. Sources indicate that adoption rates are increasing rapidly.

Problematic Examples:

Research suggests improvements. Some studies indicate benefits. Many experts agree. It has been reported that. Sources close to the matter say.

Acceptable Examples:

Johnson et al. (2023) demonstrated significant improvements. According to Dr. Smith from Harvard Medical School. The CDC reports that vaccination rates increased by 15%. A peer-reviewed study published in JAMA found.

Presuppositions Test

The politician still refuses to address the corruption scandal. Another failed attempt to fix the broken system. The CEO admits that the company's strategy was flawed. Even supporters acknowledge the policy's limitations. The organization continues to ignore mounting evidence.

Problematic Examples:

Even scientists admit climate change is debatable. Still refuses to apologize for the mistake. Another disappointing failure. Admits the policy failed. Continues to deny the obvious.

Acceptable Examples:

Even beginners can understand this concept. Still working on the complex problem. Another successful implementation. Admits the challenge is significant. Continues to research the topic.

🎭 Framing & Rhetoric Category 3/4

How issues are presented and framed to influence perception

War Metaphors Test

The war on drugs has been a battleground for decades. Politicians are attacking their opponents with devastating criticism. We must defeat climate change before it destroys our planet. The battle against poverty requires strategic planning and tactical execution. Corporate enemies are targeting our market share.

Problematic Examples:

War on homelessness. Battle against education reform. Attacking the opposition's policies. Defeat the competition. Enemy of progress.

Acceptable Examples:

The team battled for the championship. Strategic planning for the marketing campaign. Defeated the defending champions. Attacking the goal in soccer. Enemy forces in the conflict zone.

Minimizers Test

The environmental crisis is just a minor concern that slightly affects our daily lives. The economic recession is merely a small adjustment in market conditions. The healthcare problems are only temporary issues that barely impact most people. These are trivial complaints about insignificant matters.

Problematic Examples:

Just ignore the climate crisis. Only a small problem with democracy. Merely a minor healthcare issue. Slightly concerning poverty rates. Barely noticeable inequality.

Acceptable Examples:

Just click the submit button. Simply follow these three steps. Only takes five minutes to complete. Merely requires basic computer skills. Slightly adjust the temperature setting.

Maximizers Test

This is a catastrophic crisis that threatens our entire civilization. The situation is unprecedented and creates massive challenges that could destroy everything we've built. The devastating consequences will be absolutely terrible and completely overwhelming for everyone involved.

Problematic Examples:

Catastrophic meeting failure. Unprecedented lunch delay. Massive email problem. Devastating printer issue. Crisis of paper shortages.

Acceptable Examples:

Catastrophic hurricane damage. Unprecedented global pandemic. Massive earthquake in Japan. Devastating wildfire destruction. Crisis response team activated.

⚠️ Manipulation Tactics Category 4/4

Techniques designed to mislead or manipulate perception

False Balance Test

We need to present both sides of the climate change debate fairly. The vaccine controversy deserves balanced coverage of all perspectives. Evolution versus creation represents two equally valid schools of thought. Fair and balanced reporting requires equal time for flat earth theories. Both sides of the Holocaust debate merit consideration.

Problematic Examples:

Both sides of the vaccination debate. Equal time for climate denial. Fair coverage of conspiracy theories. Balanced view of scientific consensus. Two sides to established facts.

Acceptable Examples:

Both political candidates present compelling arguments. Balanced approach to budget priorities. Two legitimate strategies for implementation. Fair consideration of policy options. Both teams played exceptionally well.

Euphemisms Test

The enhanced interrogation techniques proved effective for intelligence gathering. Collateral damage was minimized during the military operation. The company is rightsizing its workforce to improve efficiency. The ethnic cleansing campaign was described as population management. Revenue enhancement strategies include new fee structures.

Problematic Examples:

Enhanced interrogation (torture). Collateral damage (civilian deaths). Rightsizing (layoffs). Ethnic cleansing (genocide). Revenue enhancement (tax increases).

Acceptable Examples:

My grandmother passed away peacefully. The restroom is down the hall. He's currently between jobs. The differently abled community. She was let go due to budget cuts.

Emotional Manipulation Test

Think of the children who will suffer from this devastating policy change. The shocking revelation will leave you absolutely heartbroken and outraged. This disgusting behavior is completely unacceptable and morally reprehensible. The terrifying consequences will destroy innocent families forever.

Problematic Examples:

Think of the children (irrelevant context). Devastating impact (no evidence). Shocking revelation (minor issue). Heartbreaking story (unrelated policy). Outrageous behavior (opinion as fact).

Acceptable Examples:

Families affected by the new healthcare policy. Significant economic impact documented in the study. Important development in climate research. Personal story illustrating the policy's effects.

Gaslighting Test

That conversation never happened, and you're completely imagining things. You're being way too sensitive about this minor issue. That's not what I said at all, and you're overreacting to the situation. You're misremembering the facts and creating problems that don't exist.

Problematic Examples:

That never happened. You're imagining things. You're being too sensitive. That's not what I said. You're overreacting.

Acceptable Examples:

I have a different recollection of the events. The evidence suggests otherwise. According to the meeting notes. That response seems disproportionate. Let me clarify what I meant.

False Dilemmas Test

Either you're with us or you're against us in this critical battle. You need to pick a side right now - there's no middle ground. This is an all or nothing situation that requires immediate action. Love America or leave it - those are your only options. You're either part of the solution or part of the problem.

Problematic Examples:

Either support the policy or oppose democracy. Pick a side on this complex issue. All or nothing approach to healthcare. Love it or leave it mentality. Solution or problem binary choice.

Acceptable Examples:

Vote yes or no on the ballot measure. Accept or decline the job offer. Turn left or right at the intersection. The switch is either on or off. The verdict is guilty or not guilty.

✨ Excellence Detection Patterns

Positive writing patterns that demonstrate high-quality communication

Clear Attribution Examples

According to Smith et al. (2023, p. 45), the implementation showed significant improvement. The World Health Organization reported on March 15, 2024, that vaccination rates increased by 23%. Dr. Johnson from Harvard Medical School stated in yesterday's press conference that the research methodology was sound. The study published in Nature (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-023-05823-0) demonstrates clear correlations.

More Attribution Examples:

As documented in the Congressional Budget Office report (CBO-2024-01), the fiscal impact reaches $2.3 billion. Professor Williams from Stanford University's Economics Department explained during the April 2024 symposium that market conditions favor innovation. The peer-reviewed analysis by Martinez and Chen (Journal of Applied Psychology, 2023, Vol. 108, No. 4) provides compelling evidence.

Nuanced Language Examples

While the data generally supports this conclusion, important exceptions include urban areas with different demographic patterns. The relationship between these variables appears complex because multiple factors influence the outcome simultaneously. This trend shows promise, though several contextual factors also play significant roles in determining success.

More Nuance Examples:

The results suggest a positive correlation, although the sample size limits our ability to generalize. This approach tends to work well in most situations, but certain conditions may require alternative strategies. The evidence indicates improvement, while acknowledging that longer-term studies are needed to confirm these findings.

Transparent Communication Examples

This analysis has several important limitations, including a small sample size and potential selection bias. I should note that my background in environmental science might influence my interpretation of these climate data. The preliminary findings require further research before we can draw definitive conclusions about causation.

More Transparency Examples:

The methodology used has constraints that readers should consider when evaluating these results. Full disclosure: this research was funded by the organization that could benefit from positive findings. The data collection process encountered some challenges that may have affected the completeness of our results.

Constructive Discourse Examples

What do you think about this alternative approach to the problem? Building on Sarah's excellent point about resource allocation, we might also consider the timing aspects. I'd really value your perspective on whether this framework addresses the concerns you raised earlier. Others might reasonably argue that the benefits outweigh the costs in this scenario.

More Discourse Examples:

How would you modify this proposal to address the implementation challenges? Your earlier suggestion about phased rollout makes a lot of sense - could we expand on that idea? I'm curious about your experience with similar initiatives and what lessons we might apply here.

Evidence-Based Examples

Data from the 2023 national survey shows that 67% of respondents reported improved outcomes after implementation. Three independent peer-reviewed studies demonstrate consistent results across different populations and geographic regions. The randomized controlled trial methodology involved 1,200 participants over six months, with 94% completion rate.

More Evidence Examples:

Statistical analysis reveals a confidence interval of 95% for the observed effect size. The longitudinal study tracked 500 participants for 18 months, controlling for age, income, and education variables. Meta-analysis of 12 studies (N=4,847) shows significant effect sizes ranging from 0.3 to 0.7.
